## **Core Reporting Components**

| Component                     | Function                                                                    |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Target Value per Month        | Comparison between total deal potential and actual revenue from **Won** deals. |
| Deal Volume Target per Month  | Comparison between the total number of offers and deals successfully marked as **Won**. |
| Monthly Target by Value (User)| Individual performance breakdown comparing revenue generated against personal quotas. |
| Monthly Target by Count (User)| Individual productivity metric comparing deal volume closed against personal goals. |

---

## **Detailed Component Breakdown**

- **Monthly Target Value Analysis**
This section visualizes the gap between expected revenue and reality. It is the primary indicator of whether the sales team is meeting the organization's financial objectives.

- **Monthly Deal Volume Tracking**
Focuses on the movement of leads through the pipeline. It helps identify if the sales process is efficient enough to convert a high percentage of opportunities into successes.

- **Individual Performance by Revenue**
Attributes monetary success to specific team members, allowing managers to identify top-tier consultants and those requiring additional tactical support.

- **Individual Productivity by Count**
Measures how active an agent is in finalizing agreements. High deal counts often indicate a high level of activity and administrative efficiency.

## **How to Analyze Target Reports**

### Data Interpretation Guide

To extract maximum value from Target Reports, consider the following:

1. **Verify the Timeframe**: Ensure you are looking at the current month or quarter to make relevant decisions.

2. **Benchmark Against Quotas**: Look at the "Won" data side-by-side with the pre-set target baseline.

3. **Identify Performance Gaps**: Note any significant deviations between different users to understand which techniques are working best.

4. **Strategic Planning**: Use these metrics to adjust next month's targets or to redistribute leads more effectively.

### Utilizing Report Insights

These reports are critical for measuring workforce effectiveness and determining if current sales strategies require adjustment.

Supervisors should use this data as the primary evidence base during evaluation meetings and performance reviews.

## **Usage Tips**

To optimize your review work:

- **Regular Synchronization**: Ensure all deal statuses are updated (Won/Lost) before running end-of-month reports.
- **Accurate Quotas**: Double-check that all agent targets have been correctly entered in the CRM settings.
- **Data-Driven Discussions**: Use these visual charts as a neutral ground for coaching and performance feedback.
- **Holistic Review**: Combine target data with **Activity Logs** to see the effort behind the results.

## **Troubleshooting**

If you experience issues with the Target Reports module:

- Refresh the page to update the real-time data visualization.
- Verify your internet connectivity for stable report generation.
- Confirm your account role (Supervisor/Manager levels are typically required for these views).
- Log out and log back in if data appears to be lagging behind actual CRM updates.
- Clear your browser's persistent cache if the interface elements display incorrectly.
- Contact the support team if specific agent data is not calculating as expected.
