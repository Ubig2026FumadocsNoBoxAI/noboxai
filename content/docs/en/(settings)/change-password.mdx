**Change Password** (Mengganti Password) is a critical security module in NoBox.AI used to update your account's access credentials and ensure the integrity of your organization's data.

This menu allows users to perform routine security updates by replacing old passwords with newer, stronger alternatives. The password update process is managed through your personal account settings, which are accessible from the system navigation.

## **How to Update Your Password**

To modify your unique NoBox.AI login credentials, follow these steps:

1. Navigate to the [id.nobox.ai](https://id.nobox.ai/Account/Login/?ReturnUrl=%2F) login portal via your web browser.
2. Log in using your registered administrative or agent account.
3. In the sidebar menu, click the [ <img src="/images/en/User.png" className="inline h-auto w-auto mx-1" alt="User Management Icon" /> ] icon to access User Management.
4. Select the **[Change Password]** menu to launch the dedicated security configuration page.

5. In the Change Password form, complete the required verification and new entry fields:

### **Password Update Form Fields**

| Field                 | Technical Description                                                                                     |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Old Password          | Enter the current password you are using to verify your identity before making security changes.         |
| New Password          | Enter the new desired password. We recommend a combination of letters, numbers, and special symbols. |
| Confirm New Password | Re-enter the new password to ensure there are no typographical errors that could lock you out.            |

6. Once all fields are validated, click **[Change Password]** to commit the new credentials to the system.

### **Post-Update Procedures**

Upon the successful update of your password, you will be automatically logged out of your current session. You must then log back in using your freshly created credentials to verify they are active.

**Security Reminder**: Memorize your new password or store it in a secure, encrypted password manager. Never share these credentials with unauthorized personnel or third-party service providers.

## **Account Security Best Practices**

To maintain maximum protection for your professional account:

- **Complex Strings**: Use a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols (e.g., #, $, !).
- **Avoid Predictability**: Do not use common terms like birth dates, pet names, or sequential number patterns (e.g., "123456").
- **Routine Rotation**: Update your password every 3 to 6 months to minimize the impact of historical data leaks.
- **Zero-Sharing Policy**: Never give your password to anyone—NoBox support staff will never ask for your plain-text password.
- **Unique Credentials**: Ensure your NoBox password is not used for other personal or social media accounts.
- **Multi-Factor Shield**: Always activate Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) if available in your tenant for an additional layer of verification.

</Tips>

## **Troubleshooting Security Updates**

- **Rule Violation**: If the system rejects a new password, ensure it meets the minimum length and complexity requirements (usually 8+ characters).
- **Mismatch Errors**: If you receive a "Passwords do not match" alert, carefully re-type the new password in both entry fields.
- **Forgotten Old Credentials**: If you cannot remember your current "Old Password," use the **[Forgot Password]** link on the main login page to perform an email-based reset.
- **Connectivity Interruptions**: Ensure a stable internet connection during the update process; a connection drop can cause the save request to time out.
- **Input Lag**: If the page fails to respond after clicking "Change Password," clear your browser cache and attempt the update in an Incognito/Private window.
- **Persistence Issues**: If you are still unable to update after several attempts, contact your system administrator to verify if there are account-level locks in place.

</Kendala>
