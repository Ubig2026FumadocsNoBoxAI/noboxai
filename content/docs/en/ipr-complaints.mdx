NoBox respects the intellectual property rights of others and we expect the users of our system to do the same. We have clear procedures for handling claims or complaints related to copyright or other intellectual property infringements occurring within our service ecosystem.

This page contains formal guidance for copyright owners to submit formal infringement reports in accordance with applicable legal provisions.

---

## **Formal Complaint Procedure**

If you believe that content on one of the NoBox services has infringed your copyright or intellectual property rights, please send a written request to the Designated Agent including the following information in full:

1. **Authority**: An electronic or physical signature of the person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
2. **Work Description**: A detailed explanation of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed.
3. **Material Identification**: The specific location (URL or screenshot) of the material considered infringing to facilitate verification by our team.
4. **Contact Information**: Your valid address, phone number, and email address for further correspondence.
5. **Good Faith Statement**: A statement that you believe use of the material is not authorized by the owner or the law.
6. **Oath of Accuracy**: A statement under penalty of perjury that the information you provide is accurate and you are the legitimate owner or an authorized representative.

## **Important Reporting Notes**

Several things to consider before submitting a report:

- **Data Validation**: Ensure you truly have proof of intellectual property ownership before reporting.
- **Legal Consequences**: Providing false statements regarding IPR infringement can result in legal liabilities or countersuits.
- **Response Time**: Our team will verify your report within 3-7 business days depending on the complexity of the submitted case.

</Tips>

## **Report Follow-up**

Once a report is received, the NoBox team will:

- Conduct an in-depth review of the reported material.
- Remove or disable access to the material if proven to be infringing.
- Contact the party that uploaded the material to provide an opportunity for clarification.
- Terminate the accounts of users proven to be repeat infringers in accordance with our Terms of Service policy.
