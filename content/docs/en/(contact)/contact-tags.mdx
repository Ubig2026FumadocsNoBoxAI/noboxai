Contact Tags is an organizational module in NoBox.AI used to classify and categorize individual customer records based on specific labels.

This feature helps users organize their contact database into a structured hierarchy, making the process of searching, filtering, and managing contacts significantly more efficient. By applying the correct tags, users can rapidly identify customer types, interaction statuses, or audience segments with precision.

## **Adding a Contact Tag**

1. To create a new organizational label, navigate to the **[Contact Tags]** menu ➔ click **[Add Contact Tag]**.
2. An **[Add Contact Tag]** dialog will appear. Enter the specific title for your new label.

## **Managing Your Tag Library**

### Editing Contact Tags

To rename an existing tag, select the target entry and modify the title in the form. Click **[Save]** to apply. The change will automatically propogate across all individual contact records currently carrying that specific tag.

### Deleting Contact Tags

Select the obsolete tag and click **[Delete]**. A confirmation prompt will appear to verify the action. Click **[Yes]** to confirm or **[No]** to cancel.

<Callout type="info" title="Important Information">
  Tag deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Ensure that the tag is no
  longer required for active campaigns or reporting filters before final
  removal.
</Callout>

## **Applying Tags to Contacts**

Contact tags can be applied directly while creating or editing an individual customer record.

1. Open the **[Contacts]** menu and select a specific contact.
2. Locate the **[Tag]** input field. A dropdown list of your previously created tags will appear.
3. Select one or more relevant tags to classify the customer.

## **Advanced Filtering via Tags**

The primary benefit of tagging is the ability to rapidly isolate specific customer segments.

1. Navigate to the **[Contacts]** dashboard and click the **[Tags]** filter button at the top of the table.
2. Select the specific tags you wish to view.
3. The table will instantly refresh to display only the contacts carrying those selected labels.

<ImageZoom
  src="/images/en/4.%20Filter%20Tag%20Kontak.png"

To remove the filter and view your complete database again, click **[Reset]** or deselect the tags from the filter list.

<Tips>
## **Tagging Strategy Tips**

- **Specific Naming**: Use clear, actionable names like "Hot Prospect," "Past Buyer," or "Active Reseller" to improve team coordination.
- **Multi-Tagging**: Don't hesitate to apply multiple tags for granular segmentation (e.g., a customer can be both "VIP" and "Region-East").
- **Promotional Targeting**: Use specific tags to build highly relevant broadcast lists for upcoming marketing campaigns.
- **Performance monitoring**: Regularly audit your tag list to delete duplicate or obsolete categories, keeping your filtering menus intuitive.
- **Lifecycle tracking**: Use tags such as "New," "Retained," or "At-Risk" to monitor the health of your customer lifecycle.

</Tips>

<Kendala>
## **Troubleshooting**

- **Tag Not Visible**: If a newly created tag doesn't appear in the contact form dropdown, refresh your browser page to sync the latest data.
- **Save Failures**: Verify your internet stability if a tag fails to save or delete from the master list.
- **Duplicate Confusion**: Ensure you don't create two tags with similar names (e.g., "VIP" and "V.I.P.") to avoid fragmentation.
- **Restricted Access**: If you cannot add or delete master tags, check with your administrator regarding your account's "Supervisor" permissions.
- **Filter Lag**: If the database fails to filter after selecting a tag, log out and log back in to refresh your active user session.
- **Display Errors**: Clear your browser cache if the tag dropdown layout appears broken or misaligned.

</Kendala>
